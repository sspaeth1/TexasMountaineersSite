By-Laws

Article 1 – Name

The name of the club shall be the Texas Mountaineers (TM)

Article 2 – Purpose

The purpose of the TM is to provide an information exchange and association for the benefit of technical rock and ice climbers and alpinists; to practice safe and efficient techniques and to practice and encourage respect and protection of the environment.

Article 3 – Membership

A. The membership requirement of the TM is that a member is in good standing with the club when an application for membership has been accepted and annual dues have been paid. Members must be 18 years of age. Children of members can only participate in club activities under the direct supervision of their parents.

B. Members must have submitted a signed liability form as prescribed in Article 5 of the bylaws.

C. To maintain membership in good standing a member must meet the requirements defined in Article 3(A) annually.

D. Only members in good standing shall have voting privileges and be able to serve on the board.

Article 4 – Dues

Dues shall be established at a figure to be determined by the board on an annual basis.

Article 5 – Release of Liability

All members must sign a release of liability in a form prescribed by the board as a prerequisite to joining and remaining a member of the TM.

Article 6 – Meetings

The TM shall hold an election of officers during the last calendar quarter of the year. The newly elected TM chairperson and other officers shall take office at the close of the annual election. The election shall be conducted via electronic media and shall be announced in the same manner. Times and locations of all meetings shall be determined by the board. General membership may request a special e-board meeting, and the e-board will decide by majority vote whether a meeting is necessary.

Article 7 – Election of Officers

The following TM officers shall be elected at the annual election; Chairperson, Vice-Chairperson, Secretary, Gear Coordinator, Training Coordinator, and Treasurer. At-large members of the e-board will also be elected for all positions other than Safety Officer and Webmaster (Article 11). The total number of at-large members will be one or three so that with the officers, webmaster and safety officer the e-board will number nine or eleven. Nominating shall be made through a nominating committee and may also be made by the membership during a period of not less than two weeks prior to the election as announced via electronic media by the board. Votes shall be tallied or verified by the secretary, and a majority of votes cast shall govern. The candidates for at-large board membership that receive the highest vote totals will be the elected board members. Should there be no clear majority for a particular office, a runoff will be held between the two candidates receiving the most votes

Article 8 –  Duties of Officers

A. The Chairperson shall preside over the meeting, arrange and publish a schedule of activities, appoint necessary and standing committees, and act as the executive officer in representing and carrying out the affairs of the TM.

B. Vice-chairperson shall perform specific duties as requested by the chairperson and act in the chairperson’s absence.

C. Secretaries shall keep all records of the TM and perform other duties as requested by the chairperson.

D. The Treasurer shall keep all monies and financial records of the TM.  These duties include collection and disposition of all monies, preparing annual financial reports, maintaining the membership roster and other duties as requested by the chairperson.

E. Gear Coordinator shall store all gear and ensure that necessary gear is safe and usable for club classes and activities. The coordinator shall also maintain records of equipment checked out to members and notify the board when gear needs to be retired and replaced or when gear is missing.

F. Training Officer shall coordinate all indoor and outdoor classes and schedule available instructors for classes. The coordinator shall also maintain records of training that the membership has completed.

Article 9 – Executive Board

The TM shall be governed by an annually elected board of officers as described in Article (7), and members at large to be elected as described in Article (7). The board shall have responsibility for the management of the TM affairs and shall have full power to act to further the purpose of the TM, including the power to establish policies, procedures, and membership

Article 10 – Committees

A. A nominating committee, appointed by the chairperson each year, shall select a slate of candidates for TM elections. One or more candidates shall be chosen to run for each office.

Article 11 – Appointed Board Members

There shall be, appointed by the Chairperson and ratified by the E-board, a Safety Officer and a Webmaster who shall be voting members of the Executive Board.

Duties of the Safety Officer include the following:

A. In cooperation with the executive board and on the advice of experienced, senior members of the Texas Mountaineers, the safety officer will maintain a record of the qualifications of all members to serve as instructors for climbing classes which are offered to the membership. Trip coordinators generally do not require any qualification as an instructor. Texas Mountaineers members acting as climbing instructors must be vetted by the safety officer as specified in this paragraph for the specific instructional purposes which are required.

B. The safety officer shall assure on a continuing basis that trips have appropriate leadership. The responsibility of the trip coordinator of any particular trip for the success, enjoyment and acceptable risk shall in no respect be diminished by reason of this provision of the by-laws.

C. The safety officer shall regularly report to the executive board and to the members of the organization on the state of safety of climbing among club members as may affect club activities. He or she shall, in particular, call to the attention of the executive board at the earliest opportunity, any high-risk practices, which come to his or her attention.

Duties of the Webmaster:

A. Webmaster shall maintain the club website and community forum. The Webmaster shall also update the website to provide links to activities.

Article 12 – Executive Board Vacancies

In the event that a member of the club’s executive board is unable to complete her or his annual term in office, her or his position on the board shall be filled in the following manner:

Succession of Officers:

A. If for any reason the club chairperson is unable to complete his or her term in office, he or she shall be succeeded by the club vice-chairperson effective immediately upon the chairperson’s termination of duties. The vice-chairperson’s position shall then be filled through an election by the e-board.

B. If for any reason, any member of the board, excluding the chairperson, is unable to complete his or her term in office, his or her position on the board shall be filled through an election by the e-board.

Announcement of Vacancy:

The chairperson shall announce board vacancies to the remaining board members electronically following notification of the vacancy. The board vacancy shall be announced to the general membership electronically.

Board Elections:

A. Following the announcement of a board vacancy to the general membership, the remaining board members shall accept nominations for the vacant position from the general membership for two weeks. All nominees shall meet the requirements of the by-laws.

B. At the next regularly scheduled executive board meeting or electronic media posting at the discretion of the chairperson, following the announcement of the vacancy, the board shall formally consider all individuals nominated by the general membership including their own nominations for the vacancy. At the close of the discussion, the executive board shall vote on the nominees. A majority vote by those board members present at the meeting, or electronically, shall determine the new member.

C. In no case shall the replacement process take less than thirty days nor exceed sixty days.

Article 13 – Expenditure of Club Funds

The following approvals and procedure shall be used for the expenditure of club funds:

A. Approval of at least one voting board member shall be obtained prior to the expenditure of club funds in the amount of less than $250.00.

B. Approval of a majority of the board shall be obtained prior to the expenditure of club funds in the amount of $250.00 or greater.

C. Club members shall obtain the approvals described in items a and b above prior to the expenditure of personal funds they will request the club to reimburse.

Article 14 – Amendments to the By-Laws

Proposed amendments shall be sent at least two weeks in advance to members in good standing for voting purposes. Votes shall be by electronic media as described in Article (6) of theTM by-laws. A 2/3 majority of all ballots cast is needed to pass the proposed amendments.
