About Us

The Texas Mountaineers is a nonprofit organization dedicated to the promotion of climbing activities, education, and stewardship in the North Texas area. We offer beginner and intermediate classes and have frequent climbing trips to climbing areas in Texas and the surrounding states.The club was originally formed in 1978 as an extension of the Dallas Sierra Club, Dallas Rock Climbing Section (DRCS). In 1988, the DRCS became the Texas Mountaineers, establishing itself as an independent organization from the Sierra Club during a period of significant growth in membership. We are now a diverse group of nearly 200 climbing enthusiasts.

When it comes to climbing, the Texas Mountaineers are a passionate climbing group with members ranging in experience from simple top-roping to big-wall climbing to big alpine climbs such as the Matterhorn in the Swiss Alps. Our local stomping grounds include places like the Wichita Mountains, Quartz Mountain, Horseshoe Canyon Ranch, Enchanted Rock, and Reimer’s Ranch. Want to go someplace else?  All you have to do is jump on our forum and plan a trip. More than likely you will find another member who will join in on the fun.

Make sure you check out the classes we offer, our club calendar, and connect with us on our FaceBook page. We’re always looking for new members passionate about climbing, so if you’re ready to take the plunge and learn how to climb, head on over to our membership page and get started.

Meet The Board Members

Chairperson:

Melissa is originally from Michigan, and moved to Texas in 2004 to attend the University of Texas at Dallas. Now, she works as a Chemist in Addison, TX. She got her start rock climbing when she took the TM beginner class in 2012 and fell in love with the sport. She served as the TM secretary for a few years and is now the TM chairperson. She has a profound love for Colorado, but since her favorite type of rock is sandstone, her favorite climbing destination is Red Rock Canyon! Locally, you can often find her at the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge or various crags in Arkansas.

Vice-Chairperson:

Don Meier was born and raised in Texas.  He started his career as an engineer graduating from Texas A&M but is currently focused on leadership development and at the 7-Eleven global headquarters.  He started climbing in 2010 when a student challenged him to travel to Lake Tahoe give the sport a try.  Like most, it didn’t take much for climbing to become a passion.  Don serves on the TM board as the vice chairperson.  His favorite type of rock is sandstone.  He claims that unlike other harder rocks that get polished over time, sandstone maintains a level of friction that is comforting when having to trust your feet.  Additionally, he prefers face/friction climbing to crack climbing.  You will regularly find him at Red Rock Canyon in Nevada and also in Colorado during the summers summiting 14ers and exploring the many crags across the state.  Locally, he prefers the sandstone of Arkansas but also frequents the other areas of Texas and Oklahoma.

Sustainable Bolt Management & At-Large Board Member:

 

After moving from Minnesota, Gary began his climbing adventures in the early 80’s. First inspired by a story about El Cap in National Geographic. Erock, on Harder Than it Looks, was his first experience with the rock, as they say “The rest is history”. He was originally a member of the Dallas Rock Climbing Section of the Sierra Club (DRCS) 1983. Then a founding member of the Texas Mountaineers in 1987. Back in the 80’s climbing on the real rock is all we had. Traditional climbing is his preference but enjoys sport climbing as well. His multi-pitch climbing has taken a back seat to the mostly single pitch lines of the past 10 years.  He put up a First Ascent on Leaning Tower in the WMWR in 1988, (Lycra Sheath, 5.10c). The first ascents continue to this day with route development in Arkansas. He has been pursuing the Colorado 14ers since 1988 by coordinating TM club trips each summer. The count is now at 32 peaks summitted. There are still a lot of locations he wants to visit in the U.S. Gary is a huge advocate of Sustainable Bolt Management, leading by example and sharing the knowledge of bolt extraction to continue to use the same bolt hole far into the future.

Sustainable Bolt Management & Safety Officer:

Kevin has lived in North Texas since 1995 and has climbed rocks with the Texas Mountaineers since 2007. He loves trad climbing on the local granite crags and makes at least one trip to climb the sandstone routes at Red Rock in Las Vegas yearly. He also huffs and puffs his way up a couple of fourteeners in Colorado each year. He is currently TM’s Safety Officer and has been a board member since 2015. While creating silicon chip layouts at work with Texas Instruments, he tries to think of ways to improve TM’s training classes and get the climbing community to be more involved and become better land stewards. He has been involved with the re-bolting efforts and trail building and cleanups in WMWR for many years.

Equipment Coordinator:

Zach is originally from Kansas City, and moved to the DFW area in 2007. Upon moving to Dallas he began a career in the Rope Access building maintenance industry. He spends his days rigging and rappelling off of many of Dallas’s iconic high-rise structures. He is currently the Director of Highpoint Access and the Texas Mountaineers Gear Coordinator. He holds the following certifications: IRATA Level 3 Rope Access Technician, OSHA 30, First Aid/CPR, PPE Competent Inspector, Confined Space Rescue Technician, and a Wilderness First Responder. Zach’s industrial rope skills seamlessly lead him to his passion of recreational climbing through the Texas Mountaineers classes. Zach enjoys plugging gear on multi pitch alpine routes as well as mountaineering in the Pacific Northwest. He has submitted Mt. Baker, Mt. Rainier and is planning on climbing Mt. Shuksan in summer 2019. When he’s not working in some big airy exposure in the city you can find him traveling to crags in Arkansas and Austin to wrestle some rock, or assisting the Texas Mountaineers Trad classes.
